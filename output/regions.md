### **Hudson Valley, New York**

* **Summary:** One of the oldest U.S. wine regions, with hybrid use dating back to the 19th century.
* **Notable Varieties:** Baco Noir, Seyval Blanc, Vidal Blanc
* **Breeding Legacy:** Adoption of French-American hybrids (Baco, Vidal), also influenced by Cornell selections.
* **Link:** [Hudson Valley Wine & Grape Association](https://hvwinegod.com/)

### **Champlain Valley (New York & Vermont)**

* **Summary:** Small but active bi-state region using ultra-hardy hybrids to overcome short seasons and bitter winters.
* **Notable Varieties:** Marquette, La Crescent, Frontenac Gris
* **Breeding Link:** Heavy reliance on Minnesota and Swenson lines; low-intervention winemaking is common.
* **Link:** [Lake Champlain Wines](https://www.lakechamplainwines.org/)

### **Vermont**

* **Summary:** Known for organic, biodynamic, and natural winemaking. Vermont producers often work with high-acid hybrids and low-intervention techniques.
* **Notable Varieties:** La Crescent, Marquette, St. Croix
* **Breeding Link:** Uses hybrids from Minnesota, Swenson, and Tom Plocher.
* **Link:** [Vermont Grape & Wine Council](https://vermontgrapeandwinecouncil.com/)

### **Northern New England (New Hampshire & Maine)**

* **Summary:** Short season, long winters; vineyards here depend on the hardiest hybrids for survival.
* **Notable Varieties:** Somerset Seedless, Frontenac, Swenson Red
* **Breeding Link:** Swenson material is key here, as well as U. Minnesota vines for resilience.
